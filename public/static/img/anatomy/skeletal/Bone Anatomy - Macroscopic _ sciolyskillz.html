Recap here:

epiphysis - expanded ends of long bones, covered in hyaline cartilage
diaphysis (shaft)
metaphysis - middle part between the end of the bone and the shaft, epiphyseal line

Bone marrow resides in the medullary cavity.

  • Red bone marrow produces blood cells.
  • Yellow bone marrow stores fat.

Note:

❝Red bone marrow is replaced by yellow bone marrow as you age. In cases of extreme blood loss, the body may willingly revert yellow bone marrow back to red bone marrow, increasing the production of blood cells.❞

Articular cartilage is a layer that covers the outside of the epiphysis joint surface. The epiphyseal line is a disc of hyaline cartilage located in the metaphysis for adult long bones.

The outer surface is covered by a white membrane – the periosteum, which is secured to the bone by collagen fibers known as Sharpey’s fibers. The inner surface is covered by the endosteum.

Osteons are long cylindrical units inside of compact areas of bones.

The Haversian canal runs through the center of each osteon. Volkmann’s canals run perpendicular to these, and allow nutrients into the bone.

The microscopic bone section will be about the inside of each bone unit.
